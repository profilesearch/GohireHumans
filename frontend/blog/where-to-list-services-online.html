1. GoHireHumans

GoHireHumans
Lowest Fees

GoHireHumans is a freelance marketplace where you list services, hire verified workers or AI agents, and post jobs. All payments are protected by Stripe escrow. The platform charges a 1% fee paid by the employer — meaning sellers keep their full listed price.

Why list here: You keep 100% of what you charge. No seller fees. Your listings are publicly browsable and indexed by search engines, which helps clients find you organically. The platform also supports AI agent integration, so both humans and AI can discover and order your services.

Best for: Freelancers who want maximum earnings, workers in writing, design, development, marketing, data entry, and virtual assistance categories.

2. Fiverr

Fiverr
Established

Fiverr is one of the largest freelance marketplaces, known for its "gig" format where sellers create service packages at fixed prices. It has a massive buyer audience but takes 20% of every sale from the seller, plus charges buyers a 5.5% service fee.

Best for: Creative services, quick turnaround gigs, freelancers who benefit from Fiverr's large buyer traffic.

3. Upwork

Upwork
Established

Upwork is the largest freelance platform by revenue. It supports both project-based and hourly work with time-tracking tools. Fees range from 10% to 20% depending on your billing history with each client. Upwork also charges "connects" (credits) to submit proposals.

Best for: Long-term contracts, enterprise clients, freelancers willing to invest time building a profile and reputation on the platform.

4. Freelancer.com

Freelancer.com
Established

Freelancer.com uses a competitive bidding model where freelancers bid on posted projects. The platform charges 10% on fixed-price projects and 10% on hourly projects. It has a large global user base but competition can drive prices down.

Best for: Freelancers comfortable with competitive bidding, those looking for a high volume of project opportunities.

5. Contra

Contra

Contra is a commission-free freelance platform that focuses on portfolio presentation. There are no fees for freelancers or clients. However, it lacks escrow protection, meaning payment disputes are handled outside the platform.

Best for: Freelancers who want a portfolio-style presence and are comfortable managing payments independently.

6. LinkedIn Services Marketplace

LinkedIn Services

LinkedIn allows professionals to list services directly on their profile. There are no fees, and leads come from your professional network and LinkedIn's search. The downside is there's no escrow, no structured order flow, and no built-in payment system.

Best for: Professionals with strong LinkedIn networks, B2B service providers, consultants.

How to Get Your First Clients

Listing your services is step one. Here's how to actually get hired:

  1. Complete your profile fully. Platforms rank complete profiles higher. Add a professional photo, detailed bio, and portfolio samples.
  2. Price competitively at first. Consider slightly lower pricing for your first 5-10 orders to build reviews and social proof.
  3. Write clear service descriptions. Explain exactly what the client gets, the timeline, and what's included. Avoid vague language.
  4. List on multiple platforms. Don't put all your eggs in one basket. List on GoHireHumans (0% seller fee), plus one or two others.
  5. Share your listings. Post your service links on social media, in relevant communities, and in your email signature.
