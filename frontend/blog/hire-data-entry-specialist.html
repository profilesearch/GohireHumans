What Does a Data Entry Specialist Do?

Data entry specialists handle the organized input, transfer, and management of data across systems. Common tasks include:

  • Database entry: Inputting records into CRM systems, spreadsheets, or databases
  • Document digitization: Converting paper records to digital formats
  • Data cleaning: Identifying and correcting errors, duplicates, and inconsistencies
  • Spreadsheet management: Organizing data in Excel, Google Sheets, or Airtable
  • Invoice processing: Entering billing data, purchase orders, and financial records
  • Web research: Collecting data from websites and entering it into structured formats
  • Transcription: Converting audio or handwritten notes into typed text
  • Email list management: Updating contact databases and mailing lists

Essential Skills to Look For

  • Typing speed: 50+ WPM minimum, 65+ WPM preferred. Speed with accuracy is non-negotiable.
  • Accuracy: 98%+ accuracy rate. Request a test task to verify before committing.
  • Software proficiency: Microsoft Excel/Google Sheets, Airtable, CRM systems (Salesforce, HubSpot)
  • Attention to detail: Ability to spot and correct errors in large datasets
  • Communication: Clear reporting on progress and questions about ambiguous entries
  • Data privacy awareness: Understanding of confidentiality when handling sensitive information

How to Evaluate Candidates

1. Use a Test Task

Before hiring, provide a small paid test task (30–60 minutes of work). Give them a sample dataset with intentional errors and see if they:

  • Complete it accurately and on time
  • Flag errors or inconsistencies they found
  • Follow formatting instructions precisely
  • Ask clarifying questions when needed

Tips for Managing Data Entry Projects

  • Provide clear templates: Give your specialist a structured format to follow. The less ambiguity, the fewer errors.
  • Use milestone payments: Break large projects into batches and review each before releasing payment. GoHireHumans supports milestone-based escrow natively.
  • Set quality checkpoints: Review the first 100 records before allowing the specialist to continue.
  • Document your standards: Create a brief style guide covering formatting, abbreviations, and edge cases.
  • Use collaborative tools: Google Sheets allows real-time collaboration and version history.
