Methodology & assumptions

  • Fiverr: 20% seller commission + 5.5% buyer service fee (no small-order fee modeled — actual is +$3 on orders < $100). Source: hireinsouth.com.
  • Upwork: 10% flat freelancer fee (post-2025 restructuring) + 5% client marketplace fee modeled. Excludes Connects (typically $0.15 each, ~5-15 per proposal). Source: Upwork's 2026 structure.
  • Toptal: ~20% effective markup based on industry estimates; actual rate is not publicly disclosed.
  • GoHireHumans: 4% employer fee, 0% seller commission, 0% buyer service fee. Stripe processing fees apply on top (industry-standard for all platforms).

FAQ

Why is GoHireHumans so much cheaper?

GoHireHumans was built in 2026 for the modern freelance economy. Lower infrastructure costs, no legacy SaaS bloat, and a leaner ops model mean we can take 4% and still run a healthy business. Our bet is that volume + great UX outperforms 25.5% rents.

Is this calculator accurate for hourly work?

It models the platform's percentage take, which applies the same way to fixed and hourly. It does NOT model Upwork's time-tracking screenshots, Fiverr's milestone holds, or other operational differences — only the cash math.

What about Connects, plan tiers, and small-order fees?

The calculator uses standard public rates. It doesn't model Upwork Connects ($0.15 each), Fiverr's $3 small-order fee on transactions under $100, or Toptal's account manager percentages. Real-world fees on those platforms are often slightly higher than what this shows.
