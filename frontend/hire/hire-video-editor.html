What Does a Video Editor Do?

A video editor transforms raw footage into polished, engaging content. From YouTube videos and social media clips to corporate presentations and commercials, video editors handle cutting, color grading, audio mixing, graphics, and effects to tell your story effectively.

  • YouTube video editing (cuts, transitions, thumbnails, subtitles)
  • Social media video content (Reels, TikTok, Stories, shorts)
  • Corporate and training videos
  • Commercial and ad production
  • Color grading and correction
  • Motion graphics and animated titles
  • Audio editing, mixing, and sound design
  • Documentary and event highlight reels

What software do most video editors use?
Professional video editors commonly use Adobe Premiere Pro, DaVinci Resolve, Final Cut Pro, and After Effects (for motion graphics). When hiring, discuss your preferred delivery format and whether the editor works with your raw footage format (4K, ProRes, RAW, etc.).
How long does video editing take?
Turnaround depends on complexity: a basic 10-minute YouTube video takes 2-5 days, social media clips take 1-2 days, and corporate videos with motion graphics can take 1-3 weeks. Set clear deadlines through GoHireHumans' milestone system so expectations are aligned from the start.
Can I hire a video editor for ongoing YouTube content?
Absolutely. Many YouTube creators hire editors on a per-video or weekly retainer basis through GoHireHumans. You can set up recurring milestones for regular content production, making it easy to maintain a consistent upload schedule.
