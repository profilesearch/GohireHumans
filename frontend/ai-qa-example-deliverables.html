What a useful human AI QA report looks like.

Buyers convert faster when they can picture the output. These examples show evidence-backed deliverables reviewers can provide without promising perfect accuracy.

Support reply QA sample

Recommendation: Revise before sending Risk: Medium Issue 1: Refund promise exceeds policy. Evidence: Policy says refunds require supervisor approval after 30 days. Suggested wording: “I can escalate this for review and follow up with available options.” Tone: Empathetic, but needs clearer next step. Escalate: Yes — billing/refund decision.

Product content QA sample

Recommendation: Revise before publishing Risk: Medium Issue 1: “Best in class” claim is unsupported. Issue 2: Battery-life number needs source or removal. Suggested correction: Replace with sourced spec or softer language. Missing info: Warranty length, compatibility, return window. Publish status: OK after claims are sourced or softened.

RAG/source QA sample

Recommendation: High risk Unsupported answer: cites Source B for a claim only found nowhere in retrieved docs. Grounded claims: 4/7 Broken/weak citations: 2 Correction: Add caveat and cite Source A paragraph 3 only for supported portion. Reviewer confidence: Medium — source set may be incomplete.

Minimum quality bar

  • Pass / revise / high-risk recommendation
  • Issue severity and location
  • Evidence links, screenshots, policy refs, or source notes
  • Suggested correction where useful
  • Confidence and uncertainty notes
